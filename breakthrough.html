The Renormalization Problem — and the Fix

Why GRAFT underperforms proxy-tuning, and why it doesn't have to

1. The Discovery

GRAFT scores 59.7% on IFEval vs proxy-tuning's 62.5%. We dug into every prompt where they disagree. The gap is 29 prompts.

Key finding: In 17 out of 18 word-choice divergences, proxy's chosen token IS in the large model's top-50. GRAFT had the right token in its candidate set but picked the wrong one.

28 out of 29 failures are scoring problems, not candidate set problems.

2. Why GRAFT Scores Wrong: The Renormalization Distortion

What proxy-tuning does (correct)

$$\text{token} = \arg\max_{y \in V} \;\; \underbrace{s_{\text{large}}(y)}_{\text{raw logit}} + \underbrace{s_{\text{ft}}(y) - s_{\text{raw}}(y)}_{\text{raw logit delta}}$$

Simple addition in raw logit space. The delta preserves the relative ranking from the small models because nothing is renormalized.

What GRAFT does (distorted)

$$\text{token} = \arg\max_{y \in S_t} \;\; \underbrace{\log p_{\text{large}}^V(y)}_{\text{log-prob (normalized over V)}} + \underbrace{\log p_{\text{ft}}^{S_t}(y) - \log p_{\text{raw}}^{S_t}(y)}_{\text{log-prob delta (normalized over S_t)}}$$

The delta is computed by normalizing $p_{\text{ft}}$ and $p_{\text{raw}}$ over just the top-50 tokens $S_t$. This changes the relative rankings within the delta.

A concrete example

Suppose the 8B-instruct model has these logits for three tokens in $S_t$:

TokenRaw logit $s_{\text{ft}}$Full-vocab $\log p_{\text{ft}}^V$Restricted $\log p_{\text{ft}}^{S_t}$
woman10.0-2.5-0.01
town9.8-2.7-0.21
village5.0-7.5-5.01

In raw logit space: woman beats town by 0.2.

In full-vocab log-prob: woman beats town by 0.2 (same gap, just shifted).

In restricted log-prob: woman beats town by 0.20 (similar — but what if $S_t$ contains other high-probability tokens that change the normalization?)

The distortion happens when the top-50 tokens have a different probability landscape than the full vocabulary. Renormalizing over $S_t$ gives more weight to tokens that are common within $S_t$ but not necessarily within $V$. This can swap close rankings.

3. The Fix: Raw Logit Restriction

The simplest fix: use proxy-tuning's formula but restrict to top-$k$.

$$\text{token} = \arg\max_{y \in S_t} \;\; s_{\text{large}}(y) + s_{\text{ft}}(y) - s_{\text{raw}}(y)$$

No renormalization. The scoring within $S_t$ is identical to proxy-tuning. The only difference is the candidate set — and we just proved that 17/18 divergent tokens are in the candidate set.

This gives us the best of both worlds:

5. Why This Matters

For theory

The probability-weighted reward concentration conjecture is validated: the reward signal is concentrated in the top-$k$ tokens. But the original GRAFT formulation distorted that signal through renormalization. The fix preserves it.

For practice

Many LLM APIs only return top-$k$ logits (not the full vocabulary). With the fix, you can do proxy-tuning through an API that returns top-50 raw logits — no need for full vocabulary access. This makes inference-time steering practical for black-box models.

For the paper

The story becomes:

  1. Proxy-tuning works by transferring a reward across models
  2. The reward is concentrated in top-$k$ by probability weighting
  3. Naive restriction (renormalized log-prob) distorts the reward — loses 3pp
  4. Raw-logit restriction preserves the reward — matches full-vocab
  5. This enables proxy-tuning through top-$k$ API access
