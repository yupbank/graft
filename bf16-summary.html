Executive Summary

We re-ran the complete GRAFT pipeline using bfloat16 (full precision) models instead of 4-bit quantized ones, and compared against the proxy-tuning baseline (Liu et al. 2024). We also evaluated all four standalone models (8B base, 8B instruct, 14B base, 14B instruct) for a complete picture.

Key headline: Both decoding-time methods dramatically improve the 14B base without any fine-tuning. Proxy-tuning (full-vocab) achieves 59.3% and GRAFT (restricted, k=50) achieves 57.1%, compared to the 14B base at 40.7% and the 14B instruct ceiling at 62.7%.

8. GRAFT vs Proxy-Tuning Baseline

We compare GRAFT (restricted renormalized delta over top-k candidates) against the original proxy-tuning method (Liu et al. 2024), which applies a full-vocabulary raw logit offset:

Proxy-tuning (Liu et al. 2024)

$$\text{score}(i) = s_{\text{large}}(i) + s_{\text{ft}}(i) - s_{\text{raw}}(i) \quad \forall\, i \in V$$

Full vocabulary (151,936 tokens), raw logits, no renormalization.

GRAFT (our method)

$$\text{score}(i) = \log p_{\text{large}}^V(i) + \lambda \cdot \delta(i) \quad i \in S_t$$

Restricted to top-k (k=50), renormalized log-probabilities for delta.

Analysis:

  • Proxy-tuning wins overall by 2.2pp (59.3% vs 57.1%). The full-vocabulary approach preserves more of the delta signal, especially for length constraints (+12pp) and punctuation (+6pp).
  • GRAFT wins on change_case (92% vs 84%) and several other categories. The restriction to top-k seems to focus the delta more effectively for case-transformation tasks.
  • Both methods dramatically outperform the 14B base (+16-19pp) with zero fine-tuning.
  • GRAFT's advantage: at inference time, it only needs logits for k=50 tokens from the small models, making it feasible for black-box APIs that only expose top-k log-probs. Proxy-tuning requires full-vocabulary logits from all models.

What bf16 buys you

  • Better distributional fidelity: the recovered distribution is 29% closer to the instruct model (JS divergence)
  • Higher top-1 agreement: 89.5% vs 85.8% across 541 prompts
  • Lower noise floor: ~0.03 nats vs ~0.1 nats, making metrics more trustworthy

  • Cost: ~4x more memory (62 GB peak vs ~18 GB for 4-bit), ~2x slower inference

10. Conclusions

Running GRAFT and proxy-tuning with bf16 models across all 541 IFEval prompts, with standalone baselines for all four models:

  1. Both decoding-time methods work: Proxy-tuning achieves 59.3% and GRAFT achieves 57.1%, both dramatically above the 14B base (40.7%), with zero fine-tuning of the large model.
  2. Proxy-tuning closes 85% of the gap to the 14B instruct ceiling (62.7%), while GRAFT closes 75%. The full-vocabulary approach preserves more signal overall.
  3. GRAFT excels in specific categories: change_case (92% vs 84%), combination (91% vs 89%), and detectable_format (61% vs 59%). The restriction acts as beneficial regularization for certain constraint types.
  4. GRAFT is more practical for black-box settings: it only needs top-k logits (k=50) from the small models, making it compatible with APIs that expose limited log-probabilities. Proxy-tuning requires full-vocabulary logits from all three models.
  5. bf16 improves both methods: 89.5% first-token match (was 85.8% with 4-bit), 5.5x lower precision noise, 17% stronger delta signal.
  6. Scale helps: 14B instruct (62.7%) slightly outperforms 8B instruct (61.6%), confirming that decoding-time methods benefit from the larger model's capacity.

The two formulas

Proxy-tuning (Liu et al. 2024):

$$p_{\text{proxy}} = \text{softmax}\bigl(s_{\text{large}} + s_{\text{ft}} - s_{\text{raw}}\bigr) \quad \text{over } V$$

GRAFT (restricted renormalized delta):

$$p_{\text{graft}} = \text{softmax}\bigl(\log p_{\text{large}}^V + \lambda \cdot (\log p_{\text{ft}}^{S_t} - \log p_{\text{raw}}^{S_t})\bigr) \quad \text{over } S_t = \text{top-}k(s_{\text{large}})$$

Proxy-tuning wins on aggregate accuracy; GRAFT wins on practicality and targeted categories. Both demonstrate that instruction-following behavior can be transferred across model sizes at decoding time, with no parameter updates to the large model.
